## 1. Matter of Z-Z-O-, 26 I&N Dec. 586 (BIA 2015) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** BIA · **year:** 2015 · **outcome:** remanded · **tags:** apelacion, standard-of-review
- **holding:** An Immigration Judge's predictive findings of what may or may not occur in the future
  are findings of fact, reviewed by the Board for CLEAR ERROR (overruling Matter of A-S-B-). The
  Board may not independently re-find facts; legal questions remain de novo. Anchor authority for
  framing every standard-of-review argument in the brief.
- **url:** https://www.justice.gov/eoir/volume-26 (Interim Decision; PDF oficial DOJ)
- Nota: la regla (holding) está verificada; usarlo como autoridad del estándar, no por su disposición.

## 2. Cole v. Holder, 659 F.3d 762 (9th Cir. 2011) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** 9th Cir. · **year:** 2011 · **outcome:** remanded · **tags:** apelacion, failure-to-consider-evidence, cat
- **holding:** Where potentially dispositive testimony and documentary evidence is submitted, the
  agency must give REASONED CONSIDERATION to it; the BIA erred by mischaracterizing the record,
  ignoring an expert entirely, and assessing CAT torture risk source-by-source instead of the
  AGGREGATE risk from all sources (police, death squads, gangs). Petition granted; remanded.
- **url:** https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/614073/cole-v-holder/

## 3. Sagaydak v. Gonzales, 405 F.3d 1035 (9th Cir. 2005) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** 9th Cir. · **year:** 2005 · **outcome:** remanded · **tags:** apelacion, failure-to-consider-evidence
- **holding:** IJs and the BIA are NOT free to ignore arguments raised by a petitioner; failure to
  address an argument requires remand for the agency to consider it. Core cite for Argument III
  (record evidence/arguments overlooked).
- **url:** https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F3/405/1035/473466/

## 4. Madrigal v. Holder, 716 F.3d 499 (9th Cir. 2013) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** 9th Cir. · **year:** 2013 · **outcome:** remanded · **tags:** apelacion, cat, acquiescence
- **holding:** CAT acquiescence: legal error to focus on the government's WILLINGNESS to control the
  torturer rather than its ABILITY to do so; an applicant need not show the entire foreign
  government would acquiesce. Former Mexican soldier targeted by a cartel; petition granted and
  remanded on CAT.
- **url:** https://www.refworld.org/jurisprudence/caselaw/usaca9/2013/en/63343

## 5. Matter of O-Z- & I-Z-, 22 I&N Dec. 23 (BIA 1998) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** BIA · **year:** 1998 · **outcome:** granted · **tags:** apelacion, cumulative-harm, state-protection
- **holding:** Incidents of harm must be considered CUMULATIVELY (beatings, vandalism, threats
  against a Jewish family in Ukraine amounted to persecution in the aggregate); repeated police
  reports producing no action showed the government was unable or unwilling to protect. Useful
  against decisions that atomize the harm.
- **url:** https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/eoir/legacy/2014/07/25/3346.pdf

## 6. Matter of A-G-G-, 25 I&N Dec. 486 (BIA 2011) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** BIA · **year:** 2011 · **outcome:** remanded · **tags:** apelacion, third-country, firm-resettlement
- **holding:** Framework for firm resettlement determinations: DHS bears the INITIAL burden of a
  prima facie showing of an OFFER of permanent resettlement (direct evidence preferred; indirect
  evidence only with sufficient clarity and force). Controls any third-country/firm-resettlement
  refutation.
- **url:** https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/eoir/legacy/2014/07/25/3713.pdf

## 7. Matter of Coelho, 20 I&N Dec. 464 (BIA 1992) — VERIFICADO (con matiz)
- **court:** BIA · **year:** 1992 · **outcome:** denied · **tags:** apelacion, motion-to-remand
- **holding:** Motions to remand are subject to the same SUBSTANTIVE requirements as motions to
  reopen (material evidence, previously unavailable). Caveat for the drafter: the oft-cited "heavy
  burden" language is contextual (212(c) discretion) — do not overstate it against the client; the
  operative standard for the brief's Motion to Remand section is 8 C.F.R. §1003.2(c).
- **url:** https://www.justice.gov/sites/default/files/eoir/legacy/2012/08/14/3172.pdf

## 8. Shrestha v. Holder, 590 F.3d 1034 (9th Cir. 2010) — VERIFICADO
- **court:** 9th Cir. · **year:** 2010 · **outcome:** denied · **tags:** apelacion, credibility, standard-of-review
- **holding:** REAL ID credibility framework: adverse credibility must rest on the TOTALITY of the
  circumstances, grounded in the record and on REAL problems with the testimony, "not mere
  trivialities"; each inconsistency relied on should be assessed in light of any explanation.
  Adverse-framework case (petition denied) — use it to show what a LAWFUL adverse finding requires
  and why this IJ's finding falls short of it.
- **url:** https://www.courtlistener.com/opinion/1434187/shrestha-v-holder/
